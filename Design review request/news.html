Study progress 9 June 2026 2 min read

5,346 families enrolled — and your 1-year visits are next

5,346 pregnancies now enrolled across Liverpool and Merseyside

We're past the halfway mark for the C-GULL recruitment target. Thank you to every family who has said yes so far — and to those who have helped spread the word in clinics and online.

Through July and August we'll be sending invitations for the 1-year follow-up visit. You'll get two date choices, by post and in this app.

What's involved in the 1-year visit

  • A 30-minute appointment at your local centre
  • A short developmental check and questionnaire on sleep and feeding
  • Optional small stool sample for the gut research strand

The 2-year follow-up — which adds a gentle language assessment — is being planned for spring 2027.

Research 28 May 2026 3 min read

What your samples are telling us about the gut and the mind

The stool samples and well-being questionnaires you've shared in the first year are powering a new strand of work with Alder Hey Children's Hospital. The team is looking at how the community of microbes in a baby's gut might shape mood, sleep and behaviour later in childhood.

Early signal, from the first 600 samples

Babies with a wider variety of gut microbes at six months tend to settle more easily at one year. It's still early days — we'll know more as your children grow up alongside the study.

Alder Hey lead on the mental-health side; the C-GULL lab runs the sequencing. Only de-identified sample data is shared between the two — nothing about you or your child.
