2-year review

Your child's 24-month review meeting

You'll be invited back to the C-GULL Research Centre with your toddler for a relaxed catch-up. It's a chance for the team to see how your child is growing and developing, gather a few more measures, and chat through what's coming next.

"Back to the Research Centre with your toddler so that we can catch up with you and see how your baby is growing and developing." C-GULL study team

What'll happen at the visit

Roughly an hour, at a pace that suits you and your toddler.

  1. A short interview

    A friendly conversation about how your child has been — sleep, feeding, milestones, family life. You set the pace; nothing is a test.

  2. Measurements and a developmental check

    Height, weight and head circumference, plus a brief play-based look at language, movement and social development.

  3. Questionnaires

    A handful of validated questionnaires covering well-being, behaviour, the home environment and any health changes since the last visit.

  4. Optional samples

    If you're happy to, the team may collect a small stool or hair sample for the gut and biomarker strands. Always optional, never a condition of taking part.

  5. Discussion of next steps

    What's coming after 24 months, which sub-studies might fit your family, and what's voluntary. No pressure to decide on the day.

What the visit looks at

Drawn from the C-GULL assessment framework.

  • Child development
  • Physical and mental health
  • Family structure
  • Home environment
  • Neighbourhood and community context

And after the 24-month review?

The study continues to follow children through childhood with lighter-touch check-ins — usually one a year. We'll write to you a few months before each one.

You may also be invited to take part in optional sub-studies on topics like the gut microbiome, early language, or sleep. Each one is described separately, and you can opt in or out at any time. As the C-GULL team put it: "these assessments are voluntary."
